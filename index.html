How to playSHAPE SHOUT!Read me out loud
1

Pair up & wall off

Each team is a Guide and an Artist, sitting back-to-back or with a barrier between the pads. From this moment on, you never peek at each other’s paper. Markers up.

2

The lightning peek

The room goes silent. The Guide flips the top shape card and gets one short peek — your table picks the seconds (classic: 10) — to study it: not just the shapes, but the positions. Centered or offset? Touching or overlapping? Then the card goes face-down. No second looks. Ever.

3

The timed chaos

Timer starts — set to whatever your table agreed (classic: 60 s). The Guide draws the picture from memory on their own pad while shouting step-by-step instructions. The Artist draws only what they hear — questions allowed (“HOW BIG IS THE CIRCLE?!”), peeking never.

4

Markers down & reveal

At the buzzer, both markers drop. Flip the card and line up all three: the original, the Guide’s drawing, and the Artist’s drawing. Prepare to be humbled.

5

Score it & swap

Score with the table below, then swap roles every round so both of you get to suffer equally. Highest grand total after the final round takes the Telepathy Trophy.

Forbidden

Real-world analogies. Your brain will beg. Do not listen.

“Draw a house with a chimney.”
“It’s an ice cream cone!”
“Make a smiley face at the bottom.”
“Like a lollipop… a tree… a SNOWMAN!”
✓

Allowed

Pure geometric & positional language. Only the Open Book.

“Large square, dead center of the page.”
“Triangle on top, sharing the square’s top edge.”
“Circle inside, touching all four sides.”
“Line from the bottom-left corner, diagonally down.”
